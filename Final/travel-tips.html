Transportation & Getting Around

üöå

Public Transportation

  • Tro-tros (shared minibuses) are cheapest but can be crowded
  • STC and other bus companies for long-distance travel
  • Always negotiate tro-tro fares before boarding
  • Metro Mass Transit buses are government-run and reliable
üöó

Taxis & Ride Sharing

  • Uber and Bolt operate in major cities
  • Always negotiate taxi fares beforehand
  • Shared taxis are common and economical
  • Private taxis are safer for late-night travel

Domestic Flights

  • Passion Air and Africa World Airlines for domestic routes
  • Book flights in advance for better prices
  • Kumasi, Tamale, and Takoradi have airports
  • Consider flights for long northern trips

Cultural Etiquette & Customs

ü§ù

Greetings

Always greet people before asking questions. Use your right hand for handshakes. Show respect to elders by greeting them first.

üëó

Dress Code

Dress modestly, especially in rural areas and religious sites. Cover shoulders and knees. Light, breathable fabrics work best.

üçΩÔ∏è

Dining Etiquette

Wash hands before eating. Use your right hand for eating. Wait for elders to start eating before you begin.

üì∑

Photography

Always ask permission before photographing people. Some may expect a small tip. Avoid photos of military/government buildings.

üéÅ

Gift Giving

Bring small gifts when visiting homes. Present and receive gifts with both hands. Gifts from your home country are especially appreciated.

üõê

Religious Respect

Ghana is religious - respect all faiths. Dress appropriately for religious sites. Remove shoes when entering mosques.

Safety & Health

üè•

Health Precautions

  • Get yellow fever vaccination (required for entry)
  • Take malaria prophylaxis and use mosquito repellent
  • Drink bottled or purified water
  • Eat at busy restaurants with high turnover
  • Bring basic first aid kit and medications
üîí

Personal Safety

  • Don't display expensive jewelry or electronics
  • Use hotel safes for valuables
  • Avoid walking alone at night
  • Stay in groups when possible
  • Keep copies of important documents
üí∞

Money Safety

  • Use ATMs inside banks or malls
  • Carry cash in multiple small amounts
  • Notify your bank about travel plans
  • Use mobile money (MoMo) for convenience
  • Bargain respectfully at markets
